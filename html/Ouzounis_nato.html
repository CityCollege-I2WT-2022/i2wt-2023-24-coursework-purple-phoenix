What's the purpose of NATO?

The North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) is a political and military alliance established to promote the security and defense of its member countries. NATO was founded on April 4, 1949, with the signing of the North Atlantic Treaty. The primary purpose of NATO is outlined in Article 5 of the treaty, which states that an armed attack against one or more of its members is considered an attack against all members, and each member agrees to take action to restore and maintain the security of the North Atlantic area.

The history of NATO

The North Atlantic Treaty Organization, commonly known as NATO, is a military and political alliance of countries that was established on April 4, 1949. The organization was founded with the primary goal of ensuring the collective defense and security of its member states. The key principle of NATO is mutual defense: an attack against one or more members is considered an attack against all members, and each member agrees to come to the aid of any other member if attacked.
