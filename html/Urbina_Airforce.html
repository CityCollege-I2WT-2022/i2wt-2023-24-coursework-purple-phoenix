Current Operations

           
           

Atlanta

           

21/09/2008

Spain decided to send a P-3 Orion aircraft belonging to the 11th Wing of the Morón de la Frontera Air Base (Seville) to the French Air Force Base BA 188 in Djibouti in the national Operation Indian Sentinel, becoming the Orion Detachment initially with 39 people, including crew and support staff.

The mission was to conduct surveillance, provide information and prevent piracy off the coast of Somalia and to protect Spanish ships navigating in the area of interest and to attend emergency calls that may occur in the area. Within Operation Indian Sentinel a total of 70 missions and 566:55 hours of flight were performed, a total of 25,800 vessels were detected and identified, and the hijacking of three merchant ships was aborted.

                           
           
           

Sangaris - MISCA

As the leader of Tactical Air Detachment (TAD) Mamba within the Spanish Air Force's 31st Wing, I played a crucial role in implementing Operations Plan (OPLAN) 0063, providing essential logistical support to the French Armed Forces engaged in Operation Sangaris and the Multinational International Support mission to the Central African Republic (MISCA).

Managing the deployment and operations of a C-130 Hercules based in Libreville, Gabon, I coordinated strategic airlift missions, primarily supporting the French forces involved in Operation Sangaris. My responsibilities included resource management, ensuring mission readiness, and fostering seamless coordination between the Spanish Armed Forces, French Armed Forces, and MISCA.

Additionally, I collaborated with the Joint Force Air Component Command (JFACC) in Lyon, serving as a Spanish liaison officer and contributing to the efficient control and programming of flight resources for France's operations in Africa. This experience underscores my proficiency in strategic planning, operational leadership, and effective multinational collaboration.

                           
           
           

Barkhane (Mali)

           

26/01/2013

On January 26, 2013, led the initial 30 components of the Air Force from Zaragoza Air Base aboard a C-130 Hercules, marking the commencement of the Tactical Aid detachment "Ivory."

After a stopover in Morón Air Base to integrate personnel from the Mobile Air Control Group (GRUMOCA), orchestrated the flight path along the coast of Mauritania and Senegal to Dakar.

The first contingent included two full Hercules crews, operating teams supporting Air Squadron Deployment (EADA) for security, an Air Medical Unit Deployment Support (UMAAD) for medical assistance, and a GRUMOCA team establishing secure communications to Spain. This contingent, later expanding to 50 individuals, played a crucial role in the inaugural air transport mission between Dakar (Senegal), Bamako (Mali), and Niamey (Niger) on January 30, facilitating the transportation of material and personnel for the French Armed Forces.

This experience highlights my leadership in mission planning, deployment coordination, and successful execution of critical air transport operations in support of international objectives.

                         
           
           

Operation Sophia (European Union Naval Force Mediterranean)

           

Led as Maritime Operations Coordinator for a critical operation spanning three phases from June 22 to October 6, 2015.

In Phase 1, focused on detecting and monitoring migration networks through information collection and sea patrols in line with international law.

Transitioning to Phase 2, directed actions to board, search, seize, and divert vessels suspected of people smuggling, following international legal protocols and UN Security Council resolutions or coastal state consent.

In Phase 3, ensured compliance with resolutions or consent, authorizing necessary measures, including the elimination or disabling of vessels involved in smuggling or trafficking within the territory of concerned states.

Demonstrated expertise in leading complex maritime operations, legal compliance, and effective international coordination.

                       
       
           

How to get in

  • Official

    For joining the army as an Official:

    • With no titulation
    • With titulation

    With no titulation


    For join with out the titulation you need to pass present the overage of highschool and the overage of the EBAU Exams; the mark is calculated this way:

    O = B*0.6 + L*0.1 + E*0.1 + H*0.1 + M*0.3 + P*0.2
    O = overage over 14.
    B = Overage of highschool.
    L = EBAU exam of literature.
    E = EBAU exam of english.
    H = EBAU exam of history.
    M = EBAU exam of maths.
    P = EBAU exam of Physics.

    When you have your overage mark you present yourself to the first phase of the contest in wich all spain can take part of, so the overage mark for passing this phase is changing every year but on 2023 was a 12 over 14.
    The second phase consist of some physical fitness test where they are going to test your physical aptitud, psychological test, and medical test.
    If you pass all those test you go to prepare yourself for 5 years in Zaragoza.

    • Joining with EBAU
    • 1º course AGM
    • 2º course AGM
    • 3º course AGM
    • 4º course AGM
    • 5º AC ESPFUN course
    • Lieutenant+ Carrear diplome

    With titulation

    For join the army with titulation you need to have a degree in something related with the science and then with the mark you pass to the contest part in wich you are going to be calificated following the next formula:

    CF = PI + PFC
    CF = Final mark.
    PI = English test. that you need to take.
    PFC = Mark of the degree.

    Then if you are selected after all the proces you go the the AGM for 3 years.
    1. With titulation
    2. 1º course AGM
    3. 2º AC ESFUN course
    4. Lieutenant

    How to get in

    First you need to choose the specialty, then you need to pass the selective phase, and then the final mark is based in this formula:

    PFC = NMB + FG + VMM
    PFC = Final grade.
    NNMB = Overage grade of highschool.
    FG = EBAU exams.
    VMM = Military merits
