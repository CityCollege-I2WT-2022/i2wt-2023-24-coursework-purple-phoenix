Bulgarian Navy

The Bulgarian Navy is a branch of the Bulgarian Armed Forces responsible for naval and maritime operations. Its primary focus is on safeguarding the country's maritime borders, contributing to NATO and EU missions, and participating in international naval exercises. The Black Sea is of strategic importance to Bulgaria, and the Bulgarian Navy plays a crucial role in maintaining security in the region. Bulgaria was involved in- Balkan Wars, World War I and World War II. The navy played a limited role, mainly protecting Bulgarian harbors and shipping

First Balkan War

Bulgaria, alongside other Balkan states (Greece, Serbia, and Montenegro), participated in the First Balkan War to counter the Ottoman Empire. Initially successful, the alliance achieved significant territorial gains against the Ottomans.

World War 1

Initially, Bulgaria maintained neutrality in World War I. However, influenced by territorial aspirations and promises of territorial gains Bulgaria later joined the Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire) in 1915.

World War 2

At the beginning of World War II, Bulgaria declared neutrality. Despite being a member of the Axis Tripartite Pact, Bulgaria maintained a policy of non-belligerence, avoiding direct participation in military operations against the Allies.
