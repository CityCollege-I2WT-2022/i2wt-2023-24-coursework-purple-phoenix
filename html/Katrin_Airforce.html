Bulgarian Airforce

History

Throughout its history, the Bulgarian Armed Forces have adapted to changing geopolitical circumstances, and the country has played roles in various military alliances and conflicts.Throughout history, the Army has played a major role in defending the country's sovereignty. Only several years after its inception in 1878, Bulgaria became a regional military power and was involved in several major wars – Serbo-Bulgarian War (1885), First Balkan War (1912–13), Second Balkan War (1913), First World War (1915–1918) and Second World War (1941–1945), during which the Army gained considerable combat experience.

Modernization

Bulgaria finds itself in a challenging economic position as the poorest country within the European Union. This economic struggle has had a notable impact on the country's efforts to modernise its armed forces. Insufficient funding has hindered Bulgaria's attempts to bring its military capabilities up to contemporary (NATO) standards,

Aircraft

Bulgarian military aviation primarily consists of aircraft acquired from other countries, and it has been a user of Soviet and Russian-made aircraft.

NATO Membership

The Bulgarian Air Force is one of the oldest air forces in Europe and the world. In recent times it has been actively taking part in numerous NATO missions and exercises in Europe. Bulgaria joined NATO in 2004, becoming a member of the alliance. This marked a significant step in aligning Bulgarian defense policies and capabilities with those of NATO.

Role

The Bulgarian Air Force plays a critical role in the defense and security of Bulgaria. Its primary mission is to protect the sovereignty of Bulgarian airspace, contribute to NATO collective defense efforts, and support national and international security objectives.

Training and Collaboration

Training and collaboration are crucial aspects of the Bulgarian Air Force's efforts to maintain readiness, enhance capabilities, and foster cooperation with other military forces, particularly within the NATO framework.
