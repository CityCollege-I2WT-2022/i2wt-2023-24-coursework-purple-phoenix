Bulgarian Army

During the socialist era in Bulgaria, which lasted from 1946 to 1989, the country was a part of the Eastern Bloc and was governed by the Bulgarian Communist Party. The armed forces during this period were referred to as the Bulgarian People's Army (BPA) or Bulgarian People's Army Ground Forces.

Structure

The Bulgarian People's Army was the combined military forces of Bulgaria during the socialist period. It included the Ground Forces, Air Forces, and Navy.

Role

The primary role of the Bulgarian People's Army was to support the socialist government, ensure internal security, and align with the defense strategies of the Warsaw Pact, a military alliance of socialist states led by the Soviet Union.
